# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: B2B decision-makers (CTOs, IT directors, heads of digital transformation, procurement leads) at mid-size and large organizations in healthcare, education, manufacturing, government, media & entertainment, and non-profit sectors, evaluating a software delivery partner for a project that is often compliance-sensitive, security-sensitive, or mission-critical. They are researching vendors, often late at night or between meetings, comparing several firms, and need to feel confident before they'll pick up the phone. Secondary: startup founders and smaller teams who need outsourcing or team augmentation and are comparing agility against the same trust signals.

## Product Purpose

Ideacomp is a Prague-based custom software development company offering AI & machine learning, cybersecurity, custom web and mobile development, cloud & DevOps, and outsourcing/team augmentation services. The site's job is to convert a skeptical, risk-averse B2B visitor into a contact-form submission by demonstrating technical range and engineering rigor across sensitive industries, without relying on client logos or testimonials (none exist yet). Success is a qualified inbound inquiry through the contact form.

## Positioning

Ideacomp turns ambitious, often compliance-sensitive problems into engineered, auditable software — systems built to hold weight, for organizations that can't afford to gamble on a vendor.

## Brand Personality

Precise, grounded, quietly ambitious. The voice reads like a senior engineer explaining a system design, not a marketer selling a dream: confident about technical range, specific about capabilities, never hyperbolic. Emotional goal for the visitor: "these people understand constraints like mine (uptime, data sensitivity, compliance) and would build something I could defend to my own stakeholders."

## Anti-references

Generic AI-SaaS landing pages (centered hero, blue-to-cyan gradient text, three identical icon cards, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section) — this is what the previous version of this exact site looked like, and it must not recur. Also avoid: Webflow-agency clichés, glassmorphism as decoration, emoji used as section iconography, stock photography of people shaking hands or pointing at laptops, testimonial/logo walls (no real proof exists yet — don't fake it with placeholders).

## Design Principles

- Engineering confidence over sales enthusiasm: copy and visuals earn trust through specificity (real capabilities, real process) rather than superlatives.
- Show the system, not a stock photo of a person near a system: prefer generated schematics/diagrams over generic imagery.
- One earned sequence: reserve numbered steps for the one place it's real (the delivery process); never decorate unrelated sections with 01/02/03.
- Design without borrowed proof: build credibility from process rigor and technical depth since no testimonials or client logos exist yet; never fabricate placeholder quotes or logos.
- Every industry named gets real specificity, not an emoji and a label.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target: minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text and 3:1 for large text/UI components, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, descriptive alt text/aria-labels on all diagrams and icons, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback for every animation, including any auto-advancing UI (which must also be user-pausable).
